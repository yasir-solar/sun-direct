What makes a responsible home solar proposal?

The strongest proposal makes the important trade-offs visible. It should show why a layout fits the property and where actual performance may vary.

  • Historical energy profile
  • Usable roof planes
  • Shading and seasonal conditions
  • Equipment and layout assumptions
  • Micro-generation requirements
  • A practical installation path

Production, savings, incentives and timelines vary by site. A property-specific assessment is required.

Questions about residential solar

Straight answers help you decide whether the next step makes sense for your property.

How do I know whether my roof gets enough sunlight for solar?+

Roof direction, pitch, nearby trees, chimneys, neighbouring buildings and usable roof area all matter. A property-specific shade and layout review is more useful than judging the roof from street level.

Can panels be installed on more than one side of my roof?+

Yes. Multiple roof planes can be used when the layout, shading and electrical design make sense. The proposal should explain how each roof section contributes to estimated annual production.

Should I size solar for an electric vehicle I plan to buy?+

Future electricity use can be considered when it is reasonably understood. Expected driving, charging habits and other planned loads should be discussed before final system sizing.

What if I sell my home after installing solar?+

The system, warranties, financing and utility arrangements should be documented clearly. Speak with your real-estate and legal professionals about how ownership or financing would be handled during a sale.

Will installation damage my shingles or cause roof leaks?+

A responsible design uses mounting and flashing methods suited to the roof type and condition. Roof condition, attachment locations and waterproofing details should be reviewed before work begins.

Can I add a battery later?+

Sometimes. Future battery readiness depends on the inverter, electrical design, available space and the equipment ecosystem selected. Mention this goal during the initial assessment even if a battery is not part of the first phase.
